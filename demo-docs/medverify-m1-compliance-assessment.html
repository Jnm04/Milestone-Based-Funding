1. Scope & Purpose

This assessment evaluates MedVerify's blockchain-based medical credential verification system against two primary regulatory frameworks: (1) the EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR, Regulation 2016/679) and (2) the EU Medical Device Regulation (MDR, Regulation 2017/745). The assessment covers data architecture, processing activities, storage mechanisms, and the classification of the software as a potential Medical Device Software (MDSW).

3. EU Medical Device Regulation (MDR) Classification

Under MDR Article 2(1) and MDCG 2019-11 guidance on MDSW classification, MedVerify was assessed against the definition of "medical device software." The platform's primary function is administrative credential verification — it does not process physiological parameters, influence clinical decisions, or perform diagnostic functions.

Classification outcome: MedVerify does NOT qualify as Medical Device Software under MDR. The platform falls within the administrative software exclusion (MDCG 2019-11, Section 3.3). No CE marking, technical file, or notified body involvement is required.

5. Recommendations & Next Steps

  • Execute Data Processing Agreements with all three hospital pilot partners before platform launch
  • Appoint a Data Protection Officer (DPO) as processing scale increases beyond threshold
  • Conduct annual DPIA review as new data categories are added
  • Register as a Trust Service Provider under eIDAS if qualified signature volume exceeds 10,000/month

6. Conclusion

MedVerify's architecture is designed with privacy-by-design principles. The separation of personal data (off-chain) from verification proof (on-chain hash) is the key architectural decision that enables GDPR compliance without compromising the immutability benefits of blockchain-based credential management. The platform is cleared to proceed to Milestone 2 (testnet smart contract deployment) from a regulatory standpoint.
